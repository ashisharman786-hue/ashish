How I Teach

Six steps from confusion to confidence

  1. 1

    Diagnostic first

    Every new student starts with a diagnostic. I map exactly what they know and where the gaps lie before teaching anything.

  2. 2

    Foundations before formulas

    I build the "why" before the "how" — so students can derive a method, not just apply it blindly.

  3. 3

    Scaffolded progression

    Difficulty rises in deliberate steps. No one is pushed to advanced problems before they are truly ready.

  4. 4

    Active recall & spaced practice

    Sessions make students retrieve and apply, not passively watch. Spaced review is built in from day one.

  5. 5

    Write like an examiner expects

    Students learn to present clear solutions and earn method marks — mark-scheme fluency alongside the maths.

  6. 6

    Two-way dialogue

    I ask as much as I answer. Explaining reasoning aloud is the deepest test of genuine understanding.
